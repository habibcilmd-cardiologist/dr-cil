{{< lead >}}
A heart attack is a serious condition that occurs when blood flow to the heart muscle is suddenly blocked. Early detection and intervention saves lives.
{{< /lead >}}

## What is a Heart Attack?

A heart attack (myocardial infarction) occurs when the coronary arteries become blocked and the heart muscle cannot receive enough oxygen. The blockage usually occurs due to a blood clot forming on the basis of atherosclerosis (hardening of the arteries).

## Main Symptoms

### 1. Chest Pain

-   Pressure or squeezing sensation in the center or left side of the chest
-   Pain lasting more than 15-20 minutes
-   Pain that doesn't go away with rest
-   Pain radiating to the arm, jaw, or back

### 2. Shortness of Breath

-   Sudden feeling of being unable to breathe
-   With or without chest pain

### 3. Other Symptoms

-   Cold sweating
-   Nausea and vomiting
-   Dizziness
-   Extreme fatigue
-   Palpitations

{{< alert "warning" >}}
**Different Symptoms in Women:** Women may experience only fatigue, nausea, or back pain without chest pain.
{{< /alert >}}

## What Should You Do?

1. **Call emergency services immediately (112 in Turkey)**
2. Chew 300 mg aspirin if available (if you're not allergic)
3. Stay calm and sit down
4. Loosen tight clothing
5. Wait for the ambulance

{{< alert "danger" >}}
**Important:** Don't try to drive yourself to the hospital when symptoms start. Call an ambulance!
{{< /alert >}}

## Risk Factors

-   High blood pressure
-   High cholesterol
-   Smoking
-   Diabetes
-   Obesity
-   Sedentary lifestyle
-   Family history
-   Stress

## Prevention

1. Exercise regularly
2. Eat healthy
3. Quit smoking
4. Keep your blood pressure under control
5. Get regular check-ups
