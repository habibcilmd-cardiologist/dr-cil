{{< lead >}}
Hypertension is one of the most important risk factors for heart disease and stroke. It's known as the silent killer because it usually has no symptoms.
{{< /lead >}}

## What is Hypertension?

Hypertension is a condition where blood pressure is consistently above normal values.

| Category             | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) |
| -------------------- | --------------- | ---------------- |
| Normal               | < 120           | < 80             |
| Elevated             | 120-129         | < 80             |
| Stage 1 Hypertension | 130-139         | 80-89            |
| Stage 2 Hypertension | ≥ 140           | ≥ 90             |

## Symptoms

Most people have no symptoms. However, in some cases:

-   Headache (especially in the morning)
-   Dizziness
-   Blurred vision
-   Shortness of breath
-   Nosebleeds

## Risk Factors

-   **Age:** Over 65
-   **Family history**
-   **Obesity**
-   **Salt consumption**
-   **Alcohol use**
-   **Smoking**
-   **Stress**
-   **Sedentary lifestyle**

## Complications

If left untreated:

-   Heart attack
-   Stroke
-   Heart failure
-   Kidney disease
-   Vision loss
-   Aortic aneurysm

## Treatment

### Lifestyle Changes

1. **DASH diet** - Fruits, vegetables, whole grains
2. **Salt restriction** - Less than 5 grams per day
3. **Regular exercise** - 150 minutes per week
4. **Weight control** - BMI 18.5-24.9
5. **Alcohol restriction**
6. **Quit smoking**
7. **Stress management**

### Medication Therapy

If lifestyle changes are not enough:

-   ACE inhibitors
-   ARBs
-   Calcium channel blockers
-   Diuretics
-   Beta blockers

{{< alert >}}
**Important:** Do not stop or change the dose of your medications without consulting your doctor.
{{< /alert >}}

## Home Blood Pressure Monitoring

1. Avoid caffeine and smoking 30 minutes before measurement
2. Rest for 5 minutes
3. Measure while sitting, with your arm at heart level
4. Measure in the morning and evening
5. Record the results
