## What is Heart Transplant?

Heart transplant is removing the diseased heart and replacing it with a healthy heart from a brain-dead donor. It is the last resort treatment in end-stage heart failure.

**Types of Heart Transplant:**

-   Orthotopic heart transplant (standard)
-   Heterotopic heart transplant (rare)

## Heart Transplant Indications

### Definite Indications

-   End-stage heart failure
-   Refractory cardiogenic shock
-   Treatment-resistant ventricular arrhythmias
-   Congenital heart disease (uncorrectable)

### Evaluation Criteria

-   Symptoms despite maximal medical therapy
-   VO2 max <12-14 ml/kg/min
-   Ejection fraction <20%
-   Recurrent hospitalizations

## Heart Transplant Contraindications

### Absolute Contraindications

-   Active infection
-   Active malignancy
-   Irreversible pulmonary hypertension
-   Severe peripheral or cerebrovascular disease

### Relative Contraindications

-   Advanced age (>70)
-   Obesity
-   Diabetes (with complications)
-   Psychosocial issues

## Heart Transplant Process

### Evaluation

-   Comprehensive cardiac evaluation
-   Right heart catheterization
-   Pulmonary vascular resistance measurement
-   Multidisciplinary evaluation

### Waiting List

-   National organ transplant waiting list
-   Ranking by urgency
-   Blood type and tissue compatibility
-   Variable waiting time

### Surgery

-   Donor heart procurement
-   Removal of patient's heart
-   Placement of new heart
-   Vascular connections

### Surgery Duration

Total duration: 4-8 hours

## Post-Heart Transplant

### Immunosuppression

-   Lifelong medication use
-   Rejection prevention
-   Infection risk
-   Regular follow-up

### Follow-up

-   Frequent endomyocardial biopsy
-   Echocardiography
-   Coronary angiography
-   Laboratory tests

## Heart Transplant Results

### Survival Rates

-   1-year survival: 85-90%
-   5-year survival: 70-75%
-   10-year survival: 50-55%

### Quality of Life

-   Symptom improvement
-   Increased exercise capacity
-   Return to normal life
-   Work and social activities
